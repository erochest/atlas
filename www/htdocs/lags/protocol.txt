		      CodeMap For a Lexical Item

			by Michael Montgomery


The DOS-based CodeMap program will produce maps like those on pages
5-14 below.

Names and numbers of LAGS data files are most conveniently found in
the Technical Index to LAGS, volume 3 of the hardback series published
by the Uniiversity of Georgia Press (Pederson, Lee, Susan Leas
McDaniel, Carol Adams, and Caisheng Liao, eds. 1989.

From the DOS prompt, type "codemap" and follow the directions below.
Further instructions are given in brackets and in comments to the
right.

A>codemap + [RETURN]

3-factor codes for 1990 version of CodeMap program, which categorizes
and maps black and white speakers into six combinations of other
social categories: sex, age, level of education, and social status.

        1: Race/Sex/Age                     See CodeMap 1, p 5
        2: Race/Sex/Education               See CodeMap 2, p 6
        3: Race/Sex/Social Status           See CodeMap 3, p 7

        6: Race/Education/Social Status     See CodeMap 6, p 10

On each CodeMap, numbers 1-4 represent and are used to map white
speakers, letters A-D are used for black speakers.
